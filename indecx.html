1. What is a Pulse?

A pulse is a single disturbance that moves through a medium (e.g., rope, slinky).

  • Medium: Substance through which the pulse travels.
  • Rest position: Undisturbed position of the medium.
  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from rest position.
  • Pulse length: Distance the pulse occupies.
  • Pulse speed (v): How fast the pulse travels.

2. Types of Pulses

  • Transverse pulse: Particles move perpendicular to wave direction (e.g., pulse on a rope).
  • Longitudinal pulse: Particles move parallel to wave direction (e.g., compression in a slinky).

3. Superposition of Pulses

  • Displacements add when pulses meet.
  • Constructive interference: Same side ‚Üí amplitudes add.
  • Destructive interference: Opposite sides ‚Üí amplitudes subtract.
  • Pulses continue in original direction after meeting.

4. Formula: Pulse Speed

v = distance / time
Example: A pulse travels 3 m in 2 s.
v = 3 / 2 = 1.5 m/s

1. Basic Concept

  • Electrostatics deals with charges at rest.
  • Like charges repel; unlike charges attract.
  • Positive: deficiency of electrons; Negative: excess of electrons.

2. Methods of Charging

  • Friction: rubbing transfers electrons.
  • Conduction: contact transfers electrons.
  • Induction: charging without contact.
